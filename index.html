Cholera (อหิวาตกโรค)

What it is, how to prevent it, and how it shaped Thailand's history. Cholera is a water- and food-borne disease caused by Vibrio cholerae, highly treatable with prompt rehydration.

Incubation: ~12h–5d Most infections: mild/asymptomatic With proper care: CFR < 1% Thai: อหิวาตกโรค

Basics

Cholera spreads through unsafe water and food. Many people show no symptoms but still shed bacteria. Symptoms typically begin 12h–5d after exposure. Early oral rehydration saves lives.

Symptoms

  • Watery diarrhea (“rice-water” stools).
  • Vomiting, thirst, cramps, dehydration.
  • Severe cases → shock & death within hours.

Prevention

Drink safe water, eat cooked food hot, wash hands with soap, and use safe sanitation. Oral cholera vaccines (OCV) help in outbreaks and endemic areas.

Treatment

Immediate rehydration (ORS, IV fluids for severe cases) saves lives. Antibiotics may be used for severe cases. With care, mortality falls below 1%.

Cholera & Thailand

Thailand faced cholera epidemics in the 19th and 20th centuries, particularly in Bangkok. Improved sanitation, water systems, and public health campaigns drastically reduced outbreaks. Today, cholera remains rare in Thailand thanks to strong prevention and treatment systems.
