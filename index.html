Cholera (อหิวาตกโรค)

Cholera is a water- and food-borne disease caused by Vibrio cholerae. Highly treatable with prompt rehydration.

Incubation: ~12h–5d Most cases mild Fatality < 1% w/ care Thai: อหิวาตกโรค

Basics

Cholera spreads through unsafe water and food. Symptoms begin 12h–5d after exposure. Rehydration saves lives.

Symptoms

  • Watery diarrhea (“rice-water” stools)
  • Vomiting, cramps, dehydration
  • Severe cases → shock, death in hours

Prevention

Drink safe water, eat hot food, wash hands with soap, use sanitation. Vaccines help in outbreaks.

Treatment

ORS or IV fluids save lives. Antibiotics may be used for severe cases. With care, mortality falls below 1%.

First Outbreaks

Cholera reached Thailand in the early 19th century, spreading along trade routes.

Bangkok Epidemics

Major outbreaks occurred in Bangkok, leading to high mortality before sanitation improved.

Modernization

Better water systems and public health campaigns reduced outbreaks across Thailand.

Rare but Monitored

Cholera is now rare in Thailand, but surveillance and vaccination remain important.
